\section*{Problem}
\begin{enumerate}
    \item \begin{enumerate}
        \item Monte Carlo is non-intrusive and dimension-independent, but the error decays only as $\mathscr{O}(N_s^{-1/2})$.
        \item Stochastic collocation and stochastic Galerkin methods both suffer from the curse of dimensionality.
    \end{enumerate}
    \item Reduced Basis methods has to choose between the low-dimensionality and expressivity of chosen type of basis.
\end{enumerate}

\section*{Key idea}
\begin{enumerate}
    \item Reduce the dimensionality of the problem by projecting the problem to a much smaller linear subspace.
    \item Opt for separate space and stochastic bassis of which both depend on time.
\end{enumerate}
